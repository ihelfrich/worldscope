## Macro Situation

The macro environment as of 27 May 2026 is best described as a late-cycle energy shock layered onto an already-stressed fiscal path, with a new Federal Reserve chair who has signaled willingness to raise rates if inflation remains elevated. The 30-year Treasury yield touched approximately 5.20% in recent weeks, its highest level since 2007, driven by the oil shock from the Hormuz closure ([CNN Business, May 19](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/19/business/30-year-treasury-yield-bond-record)). The 10-year settled near 4.50% on May 27 as markets reopened after the Memorial Day weekend, down 5-6 basis points from recent highs, but this move is more relief than resolution.

The yield curve shows persistent inversion at the 2Y/5Y segment and a steep long end: the [FRED DGS10](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/DGS10) and [DGS30](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/DGS30) series reflect the unusual configuration of a front end held down by rate-cut expectations that have since been abandoned, a 2Y-5Y range reflecting growth anxiety, and a 30Y term premium expanding on fiscal concerns. The [One Big Beautiful Bill Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_Big_Beautiful_Bill_Act) (P.L. 119-21, signed July 4, 2025) carries a [CBO-scored +$3.4 trillion to the deficit over 10 years](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/whats-in-trump-big-beautiful-bill-senate-version/), contributing structurally to yield pressure. Historically, a sustained 30Y above 5% has coincided with episodes where the US government faced meaningful bond-market pushback on fiscal trajectory: late 2023 (the "fiscal dominance" conversation) and 2007 (pre-GFC). Both episodes eventually resolved with a recession or a major policy pivot.

![Yield Curve](2026-05-27-yield_curve.png)

WTI crude at approximately $90-94/barrel (down from the April peak of ~$115 when Hormuz traffic collapsed to 5% of its pre-war average) represents partial normalization but not full recovery. The [Hormuz crisis Wikipedia page](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis) documents that 1,550+ commercial vessels were stranded at the peak with 22,500 mariners affected. That shipping disruption is still working through insurance resets, mine-clearance logistics, and tanker scheduling queues. The [UANI May 11 shipping update](https://www.unitedagainstnucleariran.com/blog/iran-war-shipping-update-may-11-2026) confirmed traffic still far below pre-war norms. Energy analysts tracking the basis between Urals (discounted Russian crude) and ESPO (eastern export route) will note that EU port restrictions on Murmansk and Tuapse could re-tighten Urals discounts if enforcement is credible.

The Federal Reserve is the institutional story of the moment. Kevin Warsh was confirmed 54-45 on May 13 in what [CNBC called the most divisive confirmation in Fed history](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/kevin-warsh-wins-senate-confirmation-as-the-next-federal-reserve-chair.html) and sworn in by Justice Clarence Thomas at the White House on May 22. His governing philosophy is sharply different from his predecessor's: he treats inflation as a choice central banks make ("the world's central bankers get to choose the inflation rate"), favors an inflation range over a rigid 2% point target, and views AI as "structurally disinflationary" - which in his framework supports holding rates higher for longer while the AI productivity wave materializes rather than cutting preemptively. The [CNBC "deep inside Wall Street's plumbing" analysis](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/kevin-warshs-real-fed-regime-change-may-happen-deep-inside-wall-streets-plumbing.html) is the best read on what "reform" means operationally: more aggressive balance sheet reduction, changes to how the Fed conducts open market operations, and reduced reliance on forward guidance. His first FOMC meeting is June 16-17.

The two May 22 Presidential Documents in the Federal Register ("Restoring Integrity to America's Financial System" and "Integrating Financial Technology Innovation Into Regulatory Frameworks") are companion executive orders that signal the administration's intention to restructure financial regulation around crypto-friendly and fintech-friendly frameworks while simultaneously pushing AML reform. The Fed's Regulation D and Regulation A proposed amendments published May 26 introduce a new "special-purpose payment account" category, which is the technical infrastructure for enabling the new stablecoin/payment framework foreshadowed in the May 22 orders.

## Cyber and Biosecurity

CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog added six entries in the May 15-22 window that together represent an unusually diverse threat surface. The most consequential for critical infrastructure:

[CVE-2026-9082](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-9082) (Drupal Core SQL Injection, added May 22): Drupal is widely deployed in US and European government, NGO, and news organization websites. A SQL injection vulnerability at this level enables authentication bypass and database exfiltration. Government website operators running Drupal core without the patch are exposed.

[CVE-2025-34291](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2025-34291) (Langflow origin validation error, added May 21): Langflow is an AI application development platform gaining rapid adoption in enterprise AI pipelines. An origin validation error in an AI orchestration tool is particularly concerning because it can enable prompt injection or model poisoning attacks through a trusted-looking input source.

[CVE-2026-20182](https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2026-20182) (Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Controller Authentication Bypass): SD-WAN controllers are high-value targets for nation-state actors because successful exploitation provides network-wide visibility and lateral movement capability. An authentication bypass at the controller level is a CISA Tier 1 priority.

The simultaneous addition of three legacy Microsoft vulnerabilities (CVE-2008-4250, CVE-2009-1537, CVE-2010-0249, CVE-2010-0806) from 2008-2010 - the original WannaCry-era unpatched pool - signals that CISA is seeing active exploitation of these known-old vulnerabilities in the wild, which typically means ransomware operators or initial-access brokers are finding unpatched legacy systems.

**2. Drupal KEV + CIRCIA timing.** CVE-2026-9082 (Drupal SQL injection, added to KEV May 22) was catalogued at the same moment that CISA announced town halls for the CIRCIA mandatory cyber incident reporting rulemaking. An organization breached via the Drupal vulnerability after CIRCIA's final rule will have mandatory reporting obligations under the new framework. **3. JEDI77 French VIP flight.** The JEDI77 callsign on May 26 ([OpenSky](https://opensky-network.org/aircraft-profile?icao24=39a842)) is not a standard French government transport prefix. **4. Langflow KEV vulnerability timing.** CVE-2025-34291 (Langflow origin validation error, added May 21) affects AI pipeline development software. Langflow is an open-source tool used to build multi-agent AI workflows. An origin validation error in this context means an attacker could inject malicious prompts or tool calls into what appears to be a legitimate trusted source. This is the first AI-pipeline-specific vulnerability in the CISA KEV catalog; its appearance signals that CISA now views AI application infrastructure as within scope for critical infrastructure vulnerability tracking - a categorical expansion.

**5. Lukashenko mobilization + Belarus emergency renewal.** The US renewed the Belarus national emergency on May 21 (published May 26) and Lukashenko announced "targeted mobilization to prepare for war" within the same week.
